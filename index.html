Eugy Han

Hi! I'm Eugy, a 4th year Ph.D. candidate at Stanford University. I'm advised by Professor Jeremy Bailenson at the Virtual Human Interaction Lab at Stanford University. Prior to Stanford, I received my B.S. in Cognitive Science at Brown University. My research focuses on how physical, virtual, and social contexts shape interactions, nonverbal behavior, and perception in virtual and mixed reality (VR/MR). I am also interested in how these research findings inform education, the law, and the design of social platforms.

  • The influence of spatial dimensions of virtual environments on attitudes and nonverbal behaviors during social interactions (2024)

    Journal of Environmental Psychology

    Han, E., DeVeaux, C., Hancock, J.T., Ram, N., Harari, G.M., & Bailenson, J.N.

    Research on physical-world environments has shown that the spatial properties of built worlds are consequential for shaping psychological states and social behavior. However, it has been difficult to empirically test this in natural settings in the physical world. This study uses immersive VR environments, which have shown to have comparable effects to physical-world environments, to investigate the influence of two spatial dimensions (ceiling height and floor area) on individuals' attitudes and nonverbal behaviors during social interactions.
